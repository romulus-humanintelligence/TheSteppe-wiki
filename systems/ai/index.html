    AI Behavior¶

    Predators, bandits, and camp NPCs use a state machine with 7 states. Herd animals have separate behavior.

    AI States¶

    1. Idle — Wanders near patrol center
    2. Patrol — Moves toward patrol point
    3. Alert — Investigates detected threat
    4. Chase — Pursues target (multi-tile movement)
    5. Attack — Engages in combat
    6. Flee — Escapes from threat
    7. Dead — No behavior

    Predator Behaviors¶

    Wolf Pack¶

    Property Value
    Alert Radius 5 tiles
    Chase Radius 8 tiles
    Aggression 0.7
    Flee HP 8
    • Alpha leads pack; charges after 2 turns in Alert
    • Pack members follow alpha's state; charge after 4 turns
    • 33% chance for non-alpha wolves to flank (move perpendicular)
    • When alpha dies: entire pack flees
    • Chase speed: 8.0 mph (vs 4.0 idle)

    Bear (Territorial)¶

    Property Value
    Alert Radius 4 tiles
    Chase Radius 6 tiles
    Aggression 0.4
    Flee HP 0 (fights to death)
    Territory Radius 5 tiles

    Warning sequence: 1. Turn 0: Territorial growl 2. Turn 2: Roar + stand on hind legs 3. Turn 4+ or distance <= 2: Charge and attack

    Won't chase beyond TerritoryRadius + 3 from home.

    Bandit Group¶

    Property Value
    Alert Radius 6 tiles
    Chase Radius 10 tiles
    Aggression 0.8
    Flee HP 10
    • Charges after 2 turns or distance <= 3
    • If equipped with bow: attempts ranged combat from 3+ tiles
    • Flee speed: 5.0 mph

    Territorial Predators¶

    Creature Alert Chase Aggression Flee HP Territory
    Caspian Tiger 6 10 0.8 10 6
    Snow Leopard 5 8 0.7 8 5
    Eurasian Lynx 5 8 0.6 5 5
    Wolverine 6 10 0.8 8 4
    Badger 5 8 0.6 5 3
    Steppe Viper 2 3 0.5 0 2

    Timid Predators¶

    Corsac Fox, Red Fox, Golden Jackal, Steppe Polecat, Golden Eagle — flee immediately on detection.

    Camp NPC Behavior¶

    Peaceful (not provoked)¶

    • Warriors wander within TerritoryRadius of camp center
    • Elders/Traders wander within 2 tiles
    • 25% chance per turn to move

    Provoked¶

    When any camp NPC is attacked, entire camp is provoked: - Warriors → Chase player - Elders/Traders → Flee

    Herd Behavior¶

    Herds use a separate system (not AI state machine): - Graze: Wander slowly at graze speed - Alert: Detected threat, freeze and watch - Flee: Run at flee speed away from threat

    Detection via VisionSystem.CanSee() + Scent System.
