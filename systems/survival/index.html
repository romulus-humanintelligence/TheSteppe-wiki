    Survival System¶

    Three core survival stats: Hunger, Thirst, and Warmth. Each drains over time and causes HP loss at zero.

    Drain Rates¶

    Stat Rate Time to Empty
    Hunger 0.0231/min ~3 days
    Thirst 0.0463/min ~1.5 days
    Warmth 0.05/min base Varies heavily

    HP Drain at Zero¶

    if Hunger == 0: HP -= 0.1/min
if Thirst == 0: HP -= 0.1/min
if Warmth == 0: HP -= 0.1/min

    Multiple zero stats stack. Starving + freezing = 0.2 HP/min.

    Warning Thresholds¶

    Stat < 40 < 25 < 10
    Hunger Getting hungry Very hungry Dying of starvation
    Thirst Getting thirsty Very thirsty Dying of thirst
    Warmth Getting cold Very cold Freezing

    Warmth Drain Chain¶

    Warmth drain is the most complex survival formula, with 8 stacking multipliers:

    warmthDrain = 0.05 × season × terrain × night × weather × windChill × fatBody × exhaustion × shelter

    1. Season Modifier¶

    Season Multiplier
    Summer 0.7x
    Spring 1.2x
    Autumn 1.3x
    Winter 2.5x

    2. Terrain Modifier¶

    Terrain Multiplier
    Dense Forest 0.70x
    Forest 0.80x
    River Bank 0.95x
    Grass 1.00x
    Scrub 1.05x
    Sand 1.10x
    Marsh 1.15x
    Hill 1.20x
    Rock 1.30x

    3. Day/Night¶

    Time Multiplier
    Day (07:00-18:00) 1.0x
    Night (20:00-05:00) 1.5x

    4. Weather¶

    See Weather for full table. Range: 1.0x (Clear) to 2.5x (Blizzard).

    5. Wind Chill¶

    Effective wind after shelter reduction:

    Wind Multiplier
    Calm 1.0x
    Light 1.1x
    Moderate 1.25x
    Strong 1.5x

    Shelter reduces wind tier: Tent/DenseForest -2, Forest -1.

    6. Low Body Fat¶

    if fat >= 40: 1.0x
if fat < 40:  1.0 + (40 - fat) / 40 × 0.5

    Range: 1.0x to 1.5x (50% more drain when starving).

    7. Exhaustion Amplifier¶

    if energy < 25: 1.0 + (25 - energy) / 25 × 0.2

    At 0 energy: 1.2x (20% increase).

    8. Shelter & Fire¶

    Player tent near fire: warmth drain reduced by fire.Heat / 100.

    Felt Blanket in inventory: 0.85x warmth drain (15% reduction).

    Interactions¶

    • Nutrition — Low body fat increases warmth drain
    • Fatigue — Exhaustion amplifies cold
    • Fire — Nearby fires restore warmth
    • Wind — Wind chill stacks with weather
